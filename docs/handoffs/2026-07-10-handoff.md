## Architectural Decisions

- **Experience entities stay factual; narrative framing belongs to future presentation/read-model layers, not the content itself.** Each `content/experiences/*.json` file holds real dates, employer/role facts, and the user's own account of accomplishments and lessons — but organizing devices like "career chapters" are explicitly kept out of the schema (see below).
- **"Career chapters" (Foundations / Entering IT / Enterprise Infrastructure) are narrative-only, not part of the domain model**, at least for now. There is no `chapter` field or entity — chapters were used only to structure today's interview and this handoff. Explicitly deferred: once all 18 entries existed, the plan was to revisit whether chapters deserve a real schema presence or should stay a computed/presentation-layer grouping. That evaluation is a natural early task for the next session, not decided today.
- **Concurrent employment is preserved, never forced into artificial chronology.** This was a hard requirement from the user for this milestone and is reflected in the raw dates on Work Market, Express Employment Professionals, and Fry's Electronics.
- **Entries emphasize growth, lessons, and business impact over a résumé-style listing.** Every entry's `summary` and `highlights` were built to surface technologies first learned, skills strengthened, leadership/communication lessons, problems solved, automation/process improvements, and how each role shaped the next stage of the career — not just job-description bullet points.
- **Public-safe filtering was applied deliberately, not automatically.** Two sensitive personal details (being near-homeless before joining RAICES; leaving RAICES for mental-health reasons) were explicitly discussed and kept out of the persisted content at the user's request, while the meaningful, mission-driven substance of that role was preserved. This is the template for any future sensitive-detail judgment calls: ask, don't assume, and don't quietly omit either — surface the tension and let the user decide.
- **Public portfolio content describes employer accomplishments and technologies, never proprietary implementation detail or private repository content.** No entry references confidential system names, internal tooling, or anything beyond what the user volunteered as safe to share.
- **Architecture and content remain separate, explicit milestones for every domain**, not one combined effort — the same split used for Profile (v0.6.0 → v0.7.0) was repeated here for Experience (v0.9.0 → v0.10.0). Recommended as the template for Skills and Projects too.
- **`districtId` assignment split by chapter, not by employer specifics.** Chapter 1 and 2 entries (customer service through early IT) are linked to `dojo-gate` (learning/discipline/mastery); Chapter 3 entries (AMETEK onward) are linked to `floating-citadel` (professional achievement) — a judgment call made collaboratively per entry, not a fixed rule enforced by schema.

## What We Learned

Recurring themes surfaced across all 18 interviews, worth remembering as the connective tissue for future Skills/Projects work and for how Mission Brief or a future Oracle might narrate this career:

- **Communication first.** From Union Bank's phone support through NDTI's executive-level technical support, clear communication is the one skill present in every single entry, regardless of how technical the role got.
- **Automation mindset.** Traceable from self-taught DOS batch/QBASIC scripts at EarthLink (2001) through PowerShell/Python/Bash/Ansible at NDTI (2024+) — a 20+ year unbroken thread, explicitly called out by the user as something to preserve.
- **Continuous learning.** Formal education (University of Phoenix) overlapping temp work, ACI Learning as a deliberate mid-career reinvestment, certifications pursued alongside full-time roles — learning never stopped being part of the job.
- **Infrastructure modernization.** A recurring pattern of taking something legacy and deliberately upgrading it: Lotus Notes → Office 365, CentOS → RHEL, XP-locked device → Windows 10, legacy domain controllers retired, Veeam upgraded.
- **Teaching and documentation.** From upselling mentorship at Union Bank, to Zendesk ticket documentation at St. Baldrick's, to IT Glue documentation at We Solve Problems, to mentoring coworkers at NDTI — teaching others is a constant, not a late-career addition.
- **Reliability and trust.** "Build systems people can trust" (NDTI) is the direct descendant of "people are frustrated with their situation, not with you" (Wescom) — the throughline is earning trust through consistent, dependable follow-through.
- **Technology exists to serve people.** Stated almost verbatim at RAICES and echoed at NDTI — the clearest single-sentence summary of the whole career arc, and arguably the thesis statement Ninjatronics itself is built on.

## Recommended Next Milestones

**v0.11.0 — Skills Domain**, connecting skills to experiences, certifications, and repositories, as proposed. This is the right next step, in this order, for a few concrete reasons:

1. **It's the most natural "connective tissue" domain left to build.** Every other domain (districts, guardians, repositories, certifications, profile, experience) exists in relative isolation today except through `districtId`. A `skill` entity with `x-ref` fields into `experience`, `certification`, and `repository` (extending the existing `relatedProjectIds` pattern on `skill.schema.json`) is what turns 18 separate work-history entries and 8 repositories into an actual graph — directly serving the "living world, not disconnected pages" vision in CLAUDE.md §1.
2. **The raw material already exists and is fresh.** Every one of today's 18 entries has a `technologies` array and highlights explicitly written to surface "skills significantly strengthened" — that's the direct input list for Skills domain content. Doing this next, while the Experience interviews are fresh, is lower-effort than coming back to it later.
3. **It's a schema change, which fits the "architecture, then content" pattern one more time** — `skill.schema.json` will likely need `relatedExperienceIds` and `relatedCertificationIds` alongside its existing `relatedProjectIds`, so this is architecture work first (v0.11.0a), then content (v0.11.0b), same shape as Profile and Experience before it.
4. **Then v0.12.0 — Projects Domain** makes sense immediately after, since `skill.relatedProjectIds` already exists in the schema today but has nothing to point to (`content/projects/` is empty) — Skills naturally motivates populating Projects next to make that reference meaningful, rather than doing Projects first and having it sit disconnected from Skills.
5. **Then v0.13.0 — Learning Roadmap**, lower urgency than Skills/Projects since nothing currently consumes it, but a clean, well-scoped domain to build once the schema → content → compiler → read-model pattern has been proven a fourth and fifth time.
6. **Then v1.0.0 — first interactive public UI**, deliberately last: every prior milestone has been verified only through `compile:world:check`, `tsc --noEmit`, and disposable scratchpad scripts — never through an actual rendered interface. Building the UI after Skills and Projects exist means the first real UI has enough of a populated world to be worth looking at, rather than rendering mostly-empty collections.
